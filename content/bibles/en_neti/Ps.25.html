Psalm 25

1 By David. O Lord, I come before you in prayer.2tn Heb “to you, O Lord, my life I lift up.” To “lift up” one’s “life” to the Lord means to express one’s trust in him through prayer. See Pss 86:4; 143:8.

2 My God, I trust in you. Please do not let me be humiliated; do not let my enemies triumphantly rejoice over me! 3 Certainly none who rely on you will be humiliated. Those who deal in treachery will be thwarted3tn Heb “those who deal in treachery in vain.” The adverb רֵיקָם (reqam, “in vain”) probably refers to the failure (or futility) of their efforts. Another option is to understand it as meaning “without cause” (cf. NIV “without excuse”; NRSV “wantonly treacherous”).
 and humiliated. 4 Make me understand your ways, O Lord! Teach me your paths!4sn Teach me your paths. In this context the Lord’s “ways” and “paths” refer to the moral principles which the Lord prescribes for his followers. See vv. 8-10.
 5 Guide me into your truth5sn The Lord’s commandments are referred to as truth here because they are a trustworthy and accurate expression of the divine will.
 and teach me. For you are the God who delivers me; on you I rely all day long. 6 Remember6tn That is, “remember” with the intention of repeating.
 your compassionate and faithful deeds, O Lord, for you have always acted in this manner.7tn Heb “for from antiquity [are] they.”
 7 Do not hold against me8tn Heb “do not remember,” with the intention of punishing.
 the sins of my youth9sn That is, the sins characteristic of youths, who lack moral discretion and wisdom.
 or my rebellious acts! Because you are faithful to me, extend to me your favor, O Lord!10tn Heb “according to your faithfulness, remember me, you, for the sake of your goodness, O Lord.”
 8 The Lord is both kind and fair;11tn Heb “good and just.”
 that is why he teaches sinners the right way to live.12tn Heb “teaches sinners in the way.”
 9 May he show13tn The prefixed verbal form is jussive; the psalmist expresses his prayer.
 the humble what is right!14tn Heb “may he guide the humble into justice.” The Hebrew term עֲנָוִים (’anavim, “humble”) usually refers to the oppressed, but in this context, where the psalmist confesses his sin and asks for moral guidance, it apparently refers to sinners who humble themselves before God and seek deliverance from their sinful condition.
 May he teach15tn The prefixed verbal form is interpreted as a jussive (it stands parallel to the jussive form, “may he guide”).
 the humble his way! 10 The Lord always proves faithful and reliable16tn Heb “all the paths of the Lord are faithful and trustworthy.” The Lord’s “paths” refer here to his characteristic actions.
 to those who follow the demands of his covenant.17tn Heb “to the ones who keep his covenant and his testimonies.”
 11 For the sake of your reputation,18tn Heb “name.” By forgiving the sinful psalmist, the Lord’s reputation as a merciful God will be enhanced.
 O Lord, forgive my sin, because it is great.19sn Forgive my sin, because it is great. The psalmist readily admits his desperate need for forgiveness.
 12 The Lord shows his faithful followers the way they should live.20tn Heb “Who is this man, the one who fears the Lord? He will instruct him in the way he should choose.” The singular (note “man”) is representative here (see v. 14, where the plural is used), and has thus been translated as a plural (“followers…they”).
 13 They experience his favor;21tn Heb “his life in goodness dwells.” The singular is representative (see v. 14).
 their descendants22tn Or “offspring”; Heb “seed.”
 inherit the land.23tn Or “earth.”
 14 The Lord’s loyal followers receive his guidance,24tn Heb “the advice of the Lord belongs to those who fear him.”
 and he reveals his covenantal demands to them.25tn Heb “and his covenant, to make them know.”
 15 I continually look to the Lord for help,26tn Heb “my eyes continually [are] toward the Lord.”
 for he will free my feet from the enemy’s net.27tn Heb “for he will bring out from a net my feet.” The hostility of the psalmist’s enemies is probably in view (see v. 19).
 16 Turn toward me and have mercy on me, for I am alone28tn That is, helpless and vulnerable.
 and oppressed! 17  Deliver me from my distress;29tc Heb “the distresses of my heart, they make wide.” The text makes little if any sense as it stands, unless this is an otherwise unattested intransitive use of the Hiphil of רָחַב (rakhav, “be wide”). It is preferable to emend the form הִרְחִיבוּ (hirkhivu; Hiphil perfect third plural “they make wide”) to הַרְחֵיב (harkhev; Hiphil imperative masculine singular “make wide”). (The final vav [ו] can be joined to the following word and taken as a conjunction.) In this case one can translate, “[in/from] the distresses of my heart, make wide [a place for me],” that is, “deliver me from the distress I am experiencing.” For the expression “make wide [a place for me],” see Ps 4:1.
 rescue me from my suffering!30tn Heb “from my distresses lead me out.”
 18 See my pain and suffering! Forgive all my sins!31tn Heb “lift up all my sins.”
 19 Watch my enemies, for they outnumber me; they hate me and want to harm me.32tn Heb “see my enemies for they are numerous, and [with] violent hatred they hate me.”
 20 Protect me33tn Or “my life.”
 and deliver me! Please do not let me be humiliated, for I have taken shelter in you! 21 May integrity and godliness protect me, for I rely on you! 22 O God, rescue34tn Or “redeem.”
 Israel from all their distress!35tn Heb “his distresses.”


sn O God, rescue Israel from all their distress. It is possible that the psalmist speaks on behalf of the nation throughout this entire psalm. Another option is that v. 22 is a later addition to the psalm which applies an original individual lament to the covenant community. If so, it may reflect an exilic setting.
