1 Praise the Lord, for it is good to sing praises to our God! Yes,2tn Or “for.”
 praise is pleasant and appropriate! 2 The Lord rebuilds Jerusalem,3map For location see Map5-B1; Map6-F3; Map7-E2; Map8-F2; Map10-B3; JP1-F4; JP2-F4; JP3-F4; JP4-F4.
 and gathers the exiles of Israel. 3 He heals4tn Heb “the one who heals.”
 the brokenhearted, and bandages their wounds. 4 He counts the number of the stars; he names all of them. 5 Our Lord is great and has awesome power;5tn Heb “and great of strength.”
 there is no limit to his wisdom.6tn Heb “to his wisdom there is no counting.”
 6 The Lord lifts up the oppressed, but knocks7tn Heb “brings down.”
 the wicked to the ground. 7 Offer to the Lord a song of thanks!8tn Heb “sing to the Lord with thanksgiving.”
 Sing praises to our God to the accompaniment of a harp! 8 He covers9tn Heb “the one who covers.”
 the sky with clouds, provides the earth with rain, and causes grass to grow on the hillsides.10tn Heb “hills.”
 9 He gives food to the animals, and to the young ravens when they chirp.11tn Heb “which cry out.”
 10 He is not enamored with the strength of a horse, nor is he impressed by the warrior’s strong legs.12tn Heb “he does not desire the strength of the horse, he does not take delight in the legs of the man.” Here “the horse” refers to the war horse used by ancient Near Eastern chariot forces, and “the man” refers to the warrior whose muscular legs epitomize his strength.
 11 The Lord takes delight in his faithful followers,13tn Heb “those who fear him.”
 and in those who wait for his loyal love. 12 Extol the Lord, O Jerusalem! Praise your God, O Zion! 13 For he makes the bars of your gates strong. He blesses your children14tn Heb “your sons.”
 within you. 14 He15tn Heb “the one who.”
 brings peace to your territory.16tn Heb “he makes your boundary peace.”
 He abundantly provides for you17tn Heb “satisfies you with.”
 the best grain. 15 He18tn Heb “the one who.”
 sends his command through the earth;19tn Heb “the one who sends his word, the earth.” The Hebrew term אֶרֶץ (’erets, “earth”) is an adverbial accusative; one must supply a preposition before it (such as “through” or “to”) in the English translation.
 swiftly his order reaches its destination.20tn Heb “swiftly his word runs.”
 16 He sends the snow that is white like wool; he spreads the frost that is white like ashes.21tn Heb “the one who gives snow like wool, frost like ashes he scatters.”
 17 He throws his hailstones22tn Heb “his ice.”
 like crumbs. Who can withstand the cold wind he sends?23tn Heb “Before his cold, who can stand?”
 18 He then orders it all to melt;24tn Heb “he sends his word and melts them.”
 he breathes on it,25tn Heb “he blows his breath.”
 and the water flows. 19 He proclaims his word to Jacob, his statutes and regulations to Israel. 20 He has not done so with any other nation; they are not aware of his regulations. Praise the Lord!
