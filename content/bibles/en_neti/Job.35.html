Hartley, Job [NICOT], 463), “I will be right before God,” or “My just cause against God.”

 6 If you sin, how does it affect God?9tn The preposition is taken here as a comparative min (מִן). The line could also read “that are high above you.” This idea has appeared in the speech of Eliphaz (22:12), Zophar (11:7ff.), and even Job (9:8ff.).
 If your transgressions are many, what does it do to him?10tn Heb “him” (also in v. 7); the referent (God) has been specified in the translation for clarity.
