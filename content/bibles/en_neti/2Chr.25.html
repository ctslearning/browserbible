Amaziah's Reign

1 Amaziah was twenty-five years old when he began to reign, and he reigned for twenty-nine years in Jerusalem.1map For location see Map5-B1; Map6-F3; Map7-E2; Map8-F2; Map10-B3; JP1-F4; JP2-F4; JP3-F4; JP4-F4.
 His mother was Jehoaddan, who was from Jerusalem. 2 He did what the Lord approved,2tn Heb “he did what was proper in the eyes of the Lord.”
 but not with wholehearted devotion.3tn Heb “a complete heart.”

3 When he had secured control of the kingdom,4tn Heb “when the kingdom was secure upon him.”
 he executed the servants who had assassinated his father.5tn Heb “he killed his servants, the ones who had struck down the king, his father.”
 4 However, he did not execute their sons. He obeyed the Lord’s commandment as recorded in the law scroll of Moses,6tn Heb “as it is written in the scroll of the law of Moses which the Lord commanded, saying.”
 “Fathers must not be executed for what their sons do,7tn Heb “on account of sons.”
 and sons must not be executed for what their fathers do.8tn Heb “on account of fathers.”
 A man must be executed only for his own sin.”9sn This law is recorded in Deut 24:16.

5 Amaziah assembled the people of Judah10tn Heb “Judah.” The words “the people of” are supplied in the translation for clarity. The Hebrew text uses the name “Judah” by metonymy here for the people of Judah.
 and assigned them by families to the commanders of units of a thousand and the commanders of units of a hundred for all Judah and Benjamin. He counted those twenty years old and up and discovered there were 300,000 young men of fighting age11tn Heb “young men going out to war.”
 equipped with spears and shields.12tn Heb “holding a spear and a shield.”
 6 He hired 100,000 Israelite warriors for a hundred talents13tn The Hebrew word כִּכַּר (kikar, “circle”) refers generally to something that is round. (58.9 kg), but later this was lowered to 108.3 lbs. (49.1 kg). More recent research suggests the “light” standard talent was 67.3 lbs. (30.6 kg). Using this as the standard for calculation, the weight of the silver was 6,730 lbs. (3,060 kg).

17 After King Amaziah of Judah consulted with his advisers,40tn The words “with his advisers” are supplied in the translation for clarification.

 18 King Joash of Israel sent this message back to King Amaziah of Judah, “A thorn bush in Lebanon sent this message to a cedar in Lebanon, ‘Give your daughter to my son as a wife.’ Then a wild animal of Lebanon came by and trampled down the thorn bush.42sn The thorn bush in the allegory is Judah. Amaziah’s success had deceived him into thinking he was on the same level as the major powers in the area (symbolized by the cedar). In reality he was not capable of withstanding an attack by a real military power such as Israel (symbolized by the wild animal).

25 King Amaziah son of Joash of Judah lived for fifteen years after the death of King Joash son of Jehoahaz of Israel. 26 The rest of the events of Amaziah’s reign, from start to finish, are recorded in the Scroll of the Kings of Judah and Israel.52tn Heb “As for the rest of the events of Amaziah, the former and the latter, are they not – behold, they are written on the scroll of the kings of Judah and Israel.”
