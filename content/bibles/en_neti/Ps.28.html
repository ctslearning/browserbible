Psalm 28

1 By David. To you, O Lord, I cry out! My protector,2tn Heb “my rocky summit.” The Lord is compared to a rocky summit where one can find protection from enemies. See Ps 18:2.
 do not ignore me!3tn Heb “do not be deaf from me.”
 If you do not respond to me,4tn Heb “lest [if] you are silent from me.”
 I will join5tn Heb “I will be equal with.”
 those who are descending into the grave.6tn Heb “the pit.” The noun בּוֹר (bor, “pit, cistern”) is sometimes used of the grave and/or the realm of the dead.

2 Hear my plea for mercy when I cry out to you for help, when I lift my hands7sn I lift my hands. Lifting one’s hands toward God was a gesture of prayer.
 toward your holy temple!8tn The Hebrew term דְּבִיר (dÿvir, “temple”) actually refers to the most holy place within the sanctuary.
 3 Do not drag me away with evil men, with those who behave wickedly,9tn Heb “workers of wickedness.”
 who talk so friendly to their neighbors,10tn Heb “speakers of peace with their neighbors.”
 while they plan to harm them!11tn Heb “and evil [is] in their heart[s].”
 4 Pay them back for their evil deeds! Pay them back for what they do! Punish them!12tn Heb “Give to them according to their work, and according to the evil of their deeds. According to the work of their hands give to them. Return their due to them.” The highly repetitive style reflects the psalmist’s agitated emotional state and draws attention to his yearning for justice.
 5 For they do not understand the Lord’s actions, or the way he carries out justice.13tn Heb “or the work of his hands.” In this context “the Lord’s actions” and “the work of his hands” probably refer to the way he carries out justice by vindicating the godly and punishing the wicked. (Note the final line of the verse, which refers to divine judgment. See also Ps 92:4-7.) Evil men do not “understand” God’s just ways; they fail to realize he will protect the innocent. Consequently they seek to harm the godly, as if they believe they will never be held accountable for their actions.
 The Lord14tn Heb “he”; the referent (the Lord, who is referred to in the two immediately preceding lines) has been specified in the translation for clarity.
 will permanently demolish them.15tn Heb “will tear them down and not rebuild them.” The ungodly are compared to a structure that is permanently demolished.
 6 The Lord deserves praise,16tn Heb “blessed [be] the Lord.”
 for he has heard my plea for mercy!17sn He has heard my plea for mercy. The psalmist’s mood abruptly changes at this point, because the Lord responded positively to his petition and assured him that he would deliver him.
 7 The Lord strengthens and protects me;18tn Heb “The Lord [is] my strength and my shield.”
 I trust in him with all my heart.19tn Heb “in him my heart trusts.”
 I am rescued20tn Or “I am helped.”
 and my heart is full of joy;21tn Heb “and my heart exults.”
 I will sing to him in gratitude.22tn Heb “and from my song I will thank him.” As pointed in the Hebrew text, מִשִּׁירִי (mishiri) appears to be “from my song,” but the preposition “from” never occurs elsewhere with the verb “to thank” (Hiphil of יָדָה, yadah). Perhaps משׁיר is a noun form meaning “song.” If so, it can be taken as an adverbial accusative, “and [with] my song I will thank him.” See P. C. Craigie, Psalms 1-50 (WBC), 236.
 8 The Lord strengthens his people;23tn Heb “the Lord [is] strength to them” (or perhaps, “to him”). The form לָמוֹ (lamo, “to them/him”) is probably a corruption of an original לְעַמוֹ (lÿ’amo, “to his people”; see P. C. Craigie, Psalms 1-50 [WBC], 236), perhaps due to quiescence of the letter ayin (ע; see P. McCarter, Textual Criticism [GBS], 55). Note the reference to the Lord’s “people” in the next verse.
 he protects and delivers his chosen king.24tn Heb “he [is] a refuge of help for his anointed one.” The noun מָשִׁיחַ (mashiakh, “anointed one”) refers to the Davidic king, who perhaps speaks as representative of the nation in this psalm. See Pss 2:2; 18:50; 20:6; 84:9; 89:38, 51; 132:10, 17.
