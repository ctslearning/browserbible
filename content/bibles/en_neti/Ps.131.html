1 A song of ascents,2sn The precise significance of this title, which appears in Pss 120-134, is unclear. Perhaps worshipers recited these psalms when they ascended the road to Jerusalem to celebrate annual religious festivals. For a discussion of their background see L. C. Allen, Psalms 101-150 (WBC), 219-21.
 by David. O Lord, my heart is not proud, nor do I have a haughty look.3tn Heb “and my eyes are not lifted up.”
 I do not have great aspirations, or concern myself with things that are beyond me.4tn Heb “I do not walk in great things, and in things too marvelous for me.”

2 Indeed5tn Or “but.”
 I am composed and quiet,6tn Heb “I make level and make quiet my soul.”
 like a young child carried by its mother;7tn Heb “like a weaned [one] upon his mother.”
 I am content like the young child I carry.8tn Heb “like the weaned [one] upon me, my soul.”
 3 O Israel, hope in the Lord now and forevermore!
