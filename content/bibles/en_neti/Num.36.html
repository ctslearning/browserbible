Women and Land Inheritance

1 Then the heads of the family groups1tn The expression is “the heads of the fathers by the family of the Gileadites.”

 and the leaders who were the heads of the Israelite families.3tn Heb “heads of the fathers.”
 2 They said, “The Lord commanded my lord to give4tn The infinitive construct “to give” serves here as the complement or object of the verb, answering what the Lord had commanded Moses.

 from another Israelite tribe, their inheritance would be taken from the inheritance of our fathers and added to the inheritance of the tribe into which they marry.6tn Heb “which they will be to them,” meaning, to those who have them, i.e., the marriages.

 their inheritance will be added to the inheritance of the tribe into which they marry. So their inheritance will be taken away from the inheritance of our ancestral tribe.”8tn Heb “the tribe of our fathers.”

Moses' Decision

5 Then Moses gave a ruling9tn Heb “commanded.”
 to the Israelites by the word10tn Heb “mouth.”
 of the Lord: “What the tribe of the Josephites is saying is right. 6 This is what11tn Heb “the word that.”
 the Lord has commanded for Zelophehad’s daughters: ‘Let them marry12tn The idiom again is “let them be for wives for….”
 whomever they think best,13tn Heb “to the one who is good in their eyes.”
 only they must marry within the family of their father’s tribe. 7 In this way the inheritance of the Israelites will not be transferred14tn Heb “turned aside.”
 from tribe to tribe. But every one of the Israelites must retain the ancestral heritage. 8 And every daughter who possesses an inheritance from any of the tribes of the Israelites must become the wife of a man from any family in her father’s tribe, so that every Israelite15tn The subject is “Israelites” and the verb is plural to agree with it, but the idea is collective as the word for “man” indicates: “so that the Israelites may possess – [each] man the inheritance of his fathers.”

10 As the Lord had commanded Moses, so the daughters of Zelophehad did. 11 For the daughters of Zelophehad– Mahlah, Tirzah, Hoglah, Milcah, and Noah– were married to the sons of their uncles.16tn They married in the family as they were instructed. But the meaning of דּוֹד (dod) is not necessarily restricted to “uncle.”
 12 They were married into the families of the Manassehites, the descendants of Joseph, and their inheritance remained in the tribe of their father’s family.
