Psalm 65

1 For the music director; a psalm of David, a song. Praise awaits you,2tn Heb “for you, silence, praise.” Many prefer to emend the noun דֻּמִיָּה (dumiyyah, “silence”) to a participle דּוֹמִיָּה (domiyyah), from the root דָּמָה (damah, “be silent”), understood here in the sense of “wait.”
 O God, in Zion. Vows made to you are fulfilled.

2 You hear prayers;3tn Heb “O one who hears prayer.”
 all people approach you.4tn Heb “to you all flesh comes.”
 3 Our record of sins overwhelms me,5tn Heb “the records of sins are too strong for me.”
 but you forgive6tn Or “make atonement for.”
 our acts of rebellion. 4 How blessed7tn The Hebrew noun is an abstract plural. The word often refers metonymically to the happiness that God-given security and prosperity produce (see Pss 1:1; 2:12; 34:9; 41:1; 84:12; 89:15; 106:3; 112:1; 127:5; 128:1; 144:15).
 is the one whom you choose, and allow to live in your palace courts.8tn Heb “[whom] you bring near [so that] he might live [in] your courts.”
 May we be satisfied with the good things of your house– your holy palace.9tn Or “temple.”
 5 You answer our prayers by performing awesome acts of deliverance, O God, our savior.10tn Heb “[with] awesome acts in deliverance you answer us, O God of our salvation.”
 All the ends of the earth trust in you,11tn Heb “a source of confidence [for] all the ends of the earth.”


sn All the ends of the earth trust in you. This idealistic portrayal of universal worship is typical hymnic hyperbole, though it does anticipate eschatological reality.
 as well as those living across the wide seas.12tc Heb “and [the] distant sea.” The plural adjective is problematic after the singular form “sea.” One could emend יָם (yam, “sea”) to יָמִים (yamim, “seas”), or emend the plural form רְחֹקִים (rÿkhoqim, “far”) to the singular רָחֹק (rakhoq). In this case the final mem (ם) could be treated as dittographic; note the mem on the beginning of the first word in v. 6.
 6 You created the mountains by your power,13tn Heb “[the] one who establishes [the] mountains by his power.”
 and demonstrated your strength.14tn Heb “one [who] is girded with strength”; or “one [who] girds himself with strength.”
 7 You calm the raging seas15tn Heb “the roar of the seas.”
 and their roaring waves, as well as the commotion made by the nations.16sn The raging seas…the commotion made by the nations. The raging seas symbolize the turbulent nations of the earth (see Ps 46:2-3, 6; Isa 17:12).
 8 Even those living in the most remote areas are awestruck by your acts;17tn Heb “and the inhabitants of the ends fear because of your signs.” God’s “signs” are the “awesome acts” (see v. 5) he performs in the earth.
 you cause those living in the east and west to praise you.18tn Heb “the goings out of the morning and the evening you cause to shout for joy.” The phrase “goings out of the morning and evening” refers to the sunrise and sunset, that is, the east and the west.
 9 You visit the earth and give it rain;19tn The verb form is a Polel from שׁוּק (shuq, “be abundant”), a verb which appears only here and in Joel 2:24 and 3:13, where it is used in the Hiphil stem and means “overflow.”
 you make it rich and fertile20tn Heb “you greatly enrich it.”
 with overflowing streams full of water.21tn Heb “[with] a channel of God full of water.” The divine name is probably used here in a superlative sense to depict a very deep stream (“a stream fit for God,” as it were).
 You provide grain for them,22tn The pronoun apparently refers to the people of the earth, mentioned in v. 8.
 for you prepare the earth to yield its crops.23tn Heb “for thus [referring to the provision of rain described in the first half of the verse] you prepare it.” The third feminine singular pronominal suffix attached to the verb “prepare” refers back to the “earth,” which is a feminine noun with regard to grammatical form.
 10 You saturate24tn Heb “saturating” [the form is an infinitive absolute].
 its furrows, and soak25tn Heb “flatten, cause to sink.”
 its plowed ground.26tn Heb “trenches,” or “furrows.”
 With rain showers you soften its soil,27tn Heb “soften it,” that is, the earth.
 and make its crops grow.28tn Heb “its vegetation you bless.” Divine “blessing” often involves endowing an object with special power or capacity.
 11 You crown the year with your good blessings,29tn Heb “your good,” which refers here to agricultural blessings.
 and you leave abundance in your wake.30tn Heb “and your paths drip with abundance.”
 12 The pastures in the wilderness glisten with moisture,31tn Heb “drip.”
 and the hills are clothed with joy.32tn That is, with rich vegetation that brings joy to those who see it.
 13 The meadows are clothed with sheep, and the valleys are covered with grain. They shout joyfully, yes, they sing.
