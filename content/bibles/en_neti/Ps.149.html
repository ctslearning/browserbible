Psalm 149

1 Praise the Lord! Sing to the Lord a new song! Praise him in the assembly of the godly!2tn Heb “his praise in the assembly of the godly ones.”
 2 Let Israel rejoice in their Creator! Let the people3tn Heb “sons.”
 of Zion delight in their king!4sn The Lord is the king here, as the parallelism in the previous line (“their creator”) indicates.
 3 Let them praise his name with dancing! Let them sing praises to him to the accompaniment of the tambourine and harp! 4 For the Lord takes delight in his people; he exalts the oppressed by delivering them.5tn Heb “he honors the oppressed [with] deliverance.”
 5 Let the godly rejoice because of their vindication!6tn Heb “in glory.” Here “glory” probably refers to the “honor” that belongs to the Lord’s people as a result of their deliverance (see v. 4).
 Let them shout for joy upon their beds!7tn The significance of the reference to “beds” is unclear. Perhaps the point is that they should rejoice at all times, even when falling asleep or awaking.
 6 May they praise God while they hold a two-edged sword in their hand,8tn Heb “[May] praises of God [be] in their throat, and a two-edged sword in their hand.”
 7 in order to take9tn Heb “to do.”
 revenge on the nations, and punish foreigners. 8 They bind10tn Heb “to bind.”
 their kings in chains, and their nobles in iron shackles, 9 and execute the judgment to which their enemies11tn Heb “they”; the referent (the enemies of the people of God) has been specified in the translation for clarity.
 have been sentenced.12tn Heb “to do against them judgment [that] is written.”
 All his loyal followers will be vindicated.13tn Heb “it is honor for all his godly ones.” The judgment of the oppressive kings will bring vindication and honor to God’s people (see vv. 4-5).
 Praise the Lord!
