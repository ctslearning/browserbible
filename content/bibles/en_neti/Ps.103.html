 3 He is the one who forgives all your sins, who heals all your diseases,4tn This relatively rare noun refers to deadly diseases (see Deut 29:22; Jer 14:18; 16:4; 2 Chr 21:19).
 4 who delivers5tn Or “redeems.”
 your life from the Pit,6tn The Hebrew term שַׁחַת (shakhat, “pit”) is often used as a title for Sheol (see Pss 16:10; 30:9; 49:9; 55:24.
 who crowns you with his loyal love and compassion, 5 who satisfies your life with good things,7tc Heb “who satisfies with the good of your ornaments.” The text as it stands makes little, if any, sense. The translation assumes an emendation of עֶדְיֵךְ (’ed’ekh, “your ornaments”) to עֹדֵכִי (’odekhiy, “your duration; your continuance”) that is, “your life” (see L. C. Allen, Psalms 101-150 [WBC], 18).
 so your youth is renewed like an eagle’s.8sn The expression your youth is renewed like an eagle’s may allude to the phenomenon of molting, whereby the eagle grows new feathers.
 6 The Lord does what is fair, and executes justice for all the oppressed.9tn Heb “the Lord does fairness, and [acts of] justice for all the oppressed.”
 7 The Lord revealed his faithful acts10tn Heb “made known his ways.” God’s “ways” in this context are his protective and salvific acts in fulfillment of his promise (see also Deut 32:4; Pss 18:30; 67:2; 77:13 [note vv. 11-12, 14]; 138:5; 145:17).
 to Moses, his deeds to the Israelites. 8 The Lord is compassionate and merciful; he is patient11tn Heb “slow to anger” (see Ps 86:15).
 and demonstrates great loyal love.12tn Heb “and great of loyal love” (see Ps 86:15).
 9 He does not always accuse, and does not stay angry.13tn The Hebrew verb נָטַר (natar) is usually taken to mean “to keep; to guard,” with “anger” being understood by ellipsis. The idiom “to guard anger” is then understood to mean “to remain angry” (see Lev 19:18; Jer 3:5, 12; Nah 1:2). However, it is possible that this is a homonymic root meaning “to be angry” (see HALOT 695 s.v. נטר).
 10 He does not deal with us as our sins deserve;14tn Heb “not according to our sins does he do to us.”
 he does not repay us as our misdeeds deserve.15tn Heb “and not according to our misdeeds does he repay us.”

 over his faithful followers.17tn Heb “those who fear him.”
 12 As far as the eastern horizon18tn Heb “sunrise.”
 is from the west,19tn Or “sunset.”
 so he removes the guilt of our rebellious actions20tn The Hebrew term פֶּשַׁע (pesha’, rebellious act”) is here used metonymically for the guilt such actions produce.
 from us. 13 As a father has compassion on his children,21tn Or “sons,” but the Hebrew term sometimes refers to children in general.
 so the Lord has compassion on his faithful followers.22tn Heb “those who fear him.”
 14 For he knows what we are made of;23tn Heb “our form.”
 he realizes24tn Heb “remembers.”

 Like a flower in the field it flourishes, 16 but when the hot wind27tn Heb “[the] wind.” The word “hot” is supplied in the translation for clarification.
 blows by, it disappears, and one can no longer even spot the place where it once grew. 17 But the Lord continually shows loyal love to his faithful followers,28tn Heb “but the loyal love of the Lord [is] from everlasting to everlasting over those who fear him.”
