The Lord Will Judge Moab

1 Here is a message about Moab: Indeed, in a night it is devastated, Ar of Moab is destroyed! Indeed, in a night it is devastated, Kir of Moab is destroyed! 2 They went up to the temple,1tn Heb “house.”
 the people of Dibon went up to the high places to lament.2tn Heb “even Dibon [to] the high places to weep.” The verb “went up” does double duty in the parallel structure.
 Because of what happened to Nebo and Medeba,3tn Heb “over [or “for”] Nebo and over [or “for”] Medeba.”
 Moab wails. Every head is shaved bare, every beard is trimmed off.4sn Shaving the head and beard were outward signs of mourning and grief.
 3 In their streets they wear sackcloth; on their roofs and in their town squares all of them wail, they fall down weeping. 4 The people of5tn The words “the people of” are supplied in the translation for clarification.
 Heshbon and Elealeh cry out, their voices are heard as far away as Jahaz. For this reason Moab’s soldiers shout in distress; their courage wavers.6tc The Hebrew text has, “For this reason the soldiers of Moab shout, his inner being quivers for him.” To achieve tighter parallelism, some emend the first line, changing חֲלֻצֵי (khalutse, “soldiers”) to חַלְצֵי (khaltse, “loins”) and יָרִיעוּ (yari’u, “they shout,” from רוּעַ, rua’) to יָרְעוּ (yor’u, “they quiver”), a verb from יָרַע (yara’), which also appears in the next line. One can then translate v. 4b as “For this reason the insides of the Moabites quiver, their whole body shakes” (cf. NAB, NRSV).
 5 My heart cries out because of Moab’s plight,7tn Heb “for Moab.” For rhetorical purposes the speaker (the Lord?, see v. 9) plays the role of a mourner.
 and for the fugitives8tn The vocalization of the Hebrew text suggests “the bars of her gates,” but the form should be repointed to yield, “her fugitives.” See HALOT 156-57 s.v. בָּרִחַ, and BDB 138 s.v. בָּרִיהַ.
 stretched out9tn The words “are stretched out” are supplied in the translation for stylistic reasons.
 as far as Zoar and Eglath Shelishiyah. For they weep as they make their way up the ascent of Luhith; they loudly lament their demise on the road to Horonaim.10tn Heb “For the ascent of Luhith, with weeping they go up it; for [on] the road to Horonaim an outcry over shattering they raise up.”
 6 For the waters of Nimrim are gone;11tn Heb “are waste places”; cf. NRSV “are a desolation.”
 the grass is dried up, the vegetation has disappeared, and there are no plants. 7 For this reason what they have made and stored up, they carry over the Stream of the Poplars. 8 Indeed, the cries of distress echo throughout Moabite territory; their wailing can be heard in Eglaim and Beer Elim.12tn Heb “to Eglaim [is] her wailing, and [to] Beer Elim [is] her wailing.”
 9 Indeed, the waters of Dimon13tc The Qumran scroll 1QIsaa reads “Dibon” instead of “Dimon” in this verse.
 are full of blood! Indeed, I will heap even more trouble on Dimon.14tn Heb “Indeed I will place on Dimon added things.” Apparently the Lord is speaking.
 A lion will attack15tn The words “will attack” are supplied in the translation for clarification.
 the Moabite fugitives and the people left in the land.
